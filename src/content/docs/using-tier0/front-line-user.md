You've been given access to a Tier0 app — a quality check, a maintenance form, a production dashboard. This page is for you: no platform theory required.

## Where your apps live

**Launchpad** is your home screen. Every app you're authorized to use appears there after it's published. Open Launchpad, pick the app, do the work.

## What you'll typically do

| Task | Example |
|---|---|
| **Fill forms** | Production reports, e-records, handover notes |
| **Run inspections** | Step-by-step checklists with pass/fail and photos |
| **Report issues** | Submit a maintenance request when a machine acts up |
| **Acknowledge tasks** | Confirm you've seen and handled an assignment |
| **Watch dashboards** | Live line status, output, and KPIs |

## Roles: who can do what

Apps are permissioned by role, so your screen shows what your job needs:

- **Field level** — collect data, complete tasks, acknowledge
- **Supervisor** — everything above, plus review, assignment, and approval
- **Management** — plant-wide KPIs, approvals, analytics

If something you need is missing, that's a permission setting — ask whoever owns the app, not IT support.

## Where your data goes (and why it matters)

Nothing you enter disappears into a form-shaped void. Every submission — a checklist result, a maintenance request, an approval — is published into the plant's shared data namespace, stamped with its context: which equipment, which line, which order, which shift.

That means:

- **Your report is traceable.** A maintenance request follows a visible workflow from submission to resolution.
- **Your data works twice.** The same inspection result feeds the quality dashboard, the weekly analysis, and the alert system — you never re-enter it.
- **The system gets smarter.** What you record becomes context the plant's analytics and AI actually use.

## Tips

- **Trust the workflow.** If the app asks for an approval step, someone is notified — no need to also message them.
- **Photos and notes help.** Downstream analysis sees what you attach.
- **Wrong data? Say so.** If a machine state or order number looks wrong on your screen, report it — it usually means a data flow upstream needs attention, and fixing it fixes it for everyone.
